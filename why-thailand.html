Thailand's Property Market

Thailand has become one of Asia's most compelling property markets for international owners. With over 40 million visitors per year and a year-round tropical climate, the country offers a rare combination of lifestyle appeal and rental income potential that few destinations can match.

A growing expat community of more than 3.5 million foreign residents has fuelled demand for quality housing, especially in key resort areas. From long-stay retirees to seasonal visitors, the diversity of residents ensures consistent occupancy across seasons.

Thailand's healthcare system is a major draw. The country is home to over 60 JCI-accredited hospitals — more than any other country in Asia — offering world-class care at a fraction of Western prices. For property owners, this translates directly to long-stay medical tourism guests and higher rental yields.

Infrastructure continues to improve year on year: new international airports, expanded motorways, high-speed rail projects, and reliable fibre internet make Thailand increasingly connected to the rest of Asia and the world. Phuket International Airport offers direct flights to major cities across Asia, making the island easily accessible for owners and guests alike.

For condominium buyers, Thailand offers one of the friendliest ownership structures in Southeast Asia. Foreign nationals can hold freehold title on condominium units outright — up to 49% of the total units in any given building — without requiring a local partner, company structure, or special permit.

Where We Operate

Regions We Serve

From beachfront villas to hillside condominiums, we manage properties across Phuket's most sought-after areas.

Phuket Town & Surrounds

The island's cultural and commercial heart. A mix of Sino-Portuguese heritage, modern shopping, and easy access to Phuket International Airport.

Central location with strong year-round occupancy from both tourists and long-stay residents. Excellent infrastructure and amenities.

Kata & Karon

Two of Phuket's most popular beach areas, known for family-friendly resorts, vibrant nightlife, and consistent waves that attract surfers in low season.

High demand from Chinese, Russian, and European tourists year-round. Strong short-term rental yields of 6-8% on well-managed properties.

Kamala & Surin

Phuket's upscale west coast. Prestigious villa estates, boutique beach clubs, and a quieter atmosphere favoured by discerning owners and high-end travellers.

Premium rental market with strong demand from affluent Asian and European guests. Higher nightly rates and excellent capital appreciation.

Rawai & Nai Harn

Phuket's relaxed southern tip. A favourite among long-stay expats, with excellent seafood markets, a laid-back village feel, and one of the island's most beautiful beaches.

Growing expat community with rising demand for quality rentals. Lower entry prices with strong long-term appreciation potential.

Long-Term Residency

Thailand Privilege Visa

Thailand's Privilege Visa program offers foreign nationals long-term residency from 5 to 20 years — with none of the complexity of traditional visa runs. It is one of the most straightforward paths to living in Southeast Asia, designed specifically for investors, retirees, and high-net-worth individuals.

Gold Membership — 5 Years

Entry-level tier with airport fast-track, annual health check, and unlimited entries. Ideal for frequent visitors and seasonal residents.

Platinum Membership — 10 Years

Extended residency with VIP airport services, government concierge, and complimentary golf and spa privileges across Thailand.

Diamond Membership — 20 Years

The ultimate tier for those committing to Thailand long-term. Full concierge, limousine transfers, and priority access to elite services.
